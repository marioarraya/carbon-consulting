### FAQ

**Section headline:** Frequently asked questions.

**Q: What does Carbon Consulting do?**
We're an IT advisory and consulting firm. We help organizations plan their technology strategy, select the right software and vendors, manage implementations, and clean up IT operations. We don't sell software or managed services — our only product is advice and project delivery.

**Q: How is this different from a managed IT provider?**
A managed IT provider runs your day-to-day IT operations. We're an advisory firm — we help you make decisions and run projects. Many of our clients already have an IT team or MSP in place; we work alongside them on the bigger strategic and project work.

**Q: How do you charge?**
Every engagement has a written proposal with a fixed fee or a not-to-exceed fee. We don't bill open-ended hourly, and we don't extend scope to extend an engagement.

**Q: How long does an engagement take?**
Most engagements run 6 to 16 weeks, depending on scope. Implementation oversight engagements can run longer — 3 to 12 months — with a regular cadence.

**Q: Do you work remotely or on-site?**
Both. Most work is done remotely, with on-site presence for kickoffs, key workshops, and executive presentations.

**Q: Who will I actually work with?**
The senior consultant you meet in the initial conversation is the one who will lead and deliver your engagement. We don't hand engagements off after the sales process.

### IT Strategy & Roadmap

**One-line summary:**
A clear technology plan that matches your business goals, budget, and timeline.

**What's included:**

- Assessment of gaps, risks, and opportunities
- Multi-year roadmap with priorities and sequencing

**Best for:**
Companies planning a major IT investment, preparing for growth, or bringing structure to reactive IT spending.

**Typical duration:** 6–10 weeks

### Software & Vendor Selection

**One-line summary:**
A structured process for choosing the right software, platform, or service provider.

**What's included:**
- Requirements gathering from end users and leadership
- Market research and shortlist development (3–5 vendors)
- Structured RFP or demo process with scoring
- Reference checks and due diligence
- Commercial analysis and contract negotiation support
- Written recommendation with scored comparison

**Common use cases:**
ERP, CRM, EHR, financial systems, HR platforms, managed service providers, core line-of-business applications.

**Typical duration:** 8–14 weeks

## Notes on What Changed

**Cut:**
- "Est. 2026 · Independent" hero tag — nobody cares when you started or that you're independent; show, don't announce.
- "The Unit of Work" section — internal firm concept, not homepage content.
- "Observed · Across a decade of engagements" quote block — no.
- Five-point "Independence — Stated, not implied" section — defensive. Folded into one line in Why Us.
- "Method" section with the quote about recommendations — removed.
- "Anatomy of an Engagement" with five phases and artifacts — removed from marketing site. This belongs in a proposal or pitch deck.
- "What You Receive" with eight numbered deliverables — removed. Pages-of-documentation descriptions don't sell; outcomes do.
- "Typical Situations" list — folded into the FAQ and the "Best for" lines under each service.

**Added (matching how real IT firms write):**
- Plain-English service names matching what clients actually search for.
- An Industries section — Power Consulting has one, and it's how buyers self-select.
- A Results section with outcome numbers — replace with real client results as they land.
- An FAQ — Power Consulting has one, and it answers the obvious questions a buyer has.
- "What's included" lists under each service — specific, scannable.
- "Best for" and "Typical duration" lines — what buyers actually want to know.

**Tone change:**
From "Technology environments do not fail in isolation" to "IT strategy, vendor selection, and project oversight — done right." The first sounds like a white paper. The second sounds like a firm you'd actually call.

## One Honest Note on Positioning

This copy positions Carbon Consulting as a standard IT advisory and consulting firm going after mid-market and enterprise buyers. That means competing against firms like Power Consulting, West Monroe, Protiviti, regional boutiques, and the advisory arms of the Big Four.

If you want a different positioning — a boutique premium firm targeting F500 CIOs for $500K+ engagements, the IT advisory equivalent of a Lazard — the register shifts. Fewer pages, less explanatory, more about named partners and engagement references. Let me know and I'll rewrite for that market instead.

But for the standard IT consulting firm path, the copy above is the right register. Direct, concrete, outcome-focused, written like you expect to be hired.
